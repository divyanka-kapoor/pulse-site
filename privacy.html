Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 12, 2026

What Pulse collects

Pulse collects only the data you provide during check-ins: your self-reported scores across nine life pillars, your life expectancy setting, and any weekly intentions you create. If you sign in, we also store your email address to identify your account.

How your data is stored

All data is stored locally on your device using AsyncStorage. If you choose to sign in with Google or Apple, your data is synced to a secure Supabase database so you can access it across devices. Data is encrypted in transit via HTTPS.

What we do not collect

  • We do not collect location data
  • We do not collect health data from Apple Health or Google Fit
  • We do not track your activity across other apps or websites
  • We do not sell, rent, or share your data with third parties
  • We do not serve ads

Third-party services

Pulse uses the following third-party services:

  • Supabase — for authentication and cloud storage (only if you sign in)
  • Google Sign-In — for authentication (only if you choose to sign in with Google)
  • Apple Sign-In — for authentication (only if you choose to sign in with Apple)
  • Groq — for optional AI-generated suggestions (no personal data is sent)

Data deletion

You can delete all your data at any time by using the reset button in the app. If you have synced data to the cloud, contact us and we will delete your account and all associated data within 30 days.

Children's privacy

Pulse is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children.

Contact

If you have questions about this policy, contact us at pulse@example.com.
